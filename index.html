üîç Understanding the Parameters

üèôÔ∏è City Selection Parameters:

‚Ä¢ GHI (Global Horizontal Irradiance): Amount of solar energy hitting a horizontal surface (W/m¬≤/day). Higher = more electricity generation.

‚Ä¢ Capacity Factor (CF): Percentage of maximum possible electricity actually generated. Accounts for day/night cycles, weather, and seasonal variations.

‚Ä¢ Why Tabuk is Best: Highest GHI (5,992 W/m¬≤/day) and best CF (28.5%) due to clear skies, low humidity, and optimal latitude.

‚öôÔ∏è Plant Configuration Parameters:

‚Ä¢ Plant Size (MW): Larger plants have economies of scale (lower cost per MW) but require more capital. 400 MW is optimal balance.

‚Ä¢ Technology Types:

  - Mono-crystalline + Tracking: Best efficiency (22.1%), follows sun movement (+25% generation)

  - Poly-crystalline Fixed: Lower cost but 8% less efficient, panels don't move

  - Bifacial + Dual Tracking: Captures light from both sides, highest generation but most expensive

  - Thin Film: Cheapest but lowest efficiency, good for large areas

‚Ä¢ Cost Scenarios:

  - Optimistic: Future cost reductions (15% lower CAPEX, 10% lower OPEX)

  - Current: Today's market prices (baseline for comparison)

  - Conservative: Higher costs accounting for risks (20% higher CAPEX, 15% higher OPEX)

üìä Economic Results Explained:

‚Ä¢ LCOE (Levelized Cost of Energy): True cost per MWh over 25 years. Includes ALL costs: construction, operations, financing, maintenance.

‚Ä¢ NPV (Net Present Value): Total profit in today's money. Positive NPV = profitable project. Higher NPV = better investment.

‚Ä¢ IRR (Internal Rate of Return): Annual profit rate. Compare to bank interest (2-4%) and stock market (8-10%). Our 14.2% is excellent!

‚Ä¢ Payback Period: Years to recover initial investment. Solar typically 7-12 years, then 13-18 years of pure profit.

‚Ä¢ CO‚ÇÇ Avoided: Environmental benefit by replacing natural gas plants. 1 MWh solar = 0.65 tons CO‚ÇÇ saved.

‚Ä¢ Jobs Created: Direct construction + operation jobs, plus indirect supply chain jobs. Multiplier effect: 1 direct job = 2.8 total jobs.

1. üí∞ LCOE Calculation (Professor Bollino's Framework)

LCOE = (Annual Capital Cost + Annual OPEX) / Annual Generation

Step-by-Step Calculation:
1. Calculate Capital Recovery Factor (CRF):
   CRF = r(1+r)‚Åø / [(1+r)‚Åø - 1]
   Where: r = 8.5% (discount rate), n = 25 years
   CRF = 0.0956 (converts $200M to $19.12M/year)

2. Annual Capital Cost = $200M √ó 0.0956 = $19.12M
3. Annual OPEX = $5.4M (operations & maintenance)
4. Annual Generation = 400MW √ó 28.5% √ó 8760h = 998,000 MWh
5. LCOE = ($19.12M + $5.4M) / 998,000 MWh = $24.6/MWh

This method accounts for the time value of money - $1 today is worth more than $1 in 25 years due to inflation and opportunity cost.
